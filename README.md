# Agentic AI

## Overview

Agentic AI refers to artificial intelligence systems that can **act autonomously**, **make decisions**, and **pursue goals** by reasoning, planning, and interacting with tools or environments.  
Unlike traditional AI models that only respond to prompts, **agentic systems take initiative** — they decide *what to do next*.

This repository is intended as a **learning and experimentation space** for understanding, building, and exploring Agentic AI concepts using code.

---

## What is Agentic AI?

An **AI agent** typically has the following characteristics:

- **Goal-oriented behavior**  
  The agent works toward an objective rather than responding once.

- **Autonomy**  
  It can decide actions without continuous human input.

- **Reasoning & Planning**  
  Breaks problems into steps and plans execution.

- **Memory**  
  Retains context or past interactions to improve decisions.

- **Tool Use**  
  Can call external tools (APIs, files, code, web search, etc.).

Agentic AI systems are often built on top of **Large Language Models (LLMs)** but extend them with control loops, state, and tools.

---

## Why Agentic AI Matters

Agentic AI enables:

- Autonomous task execution
- Multi-step problem solving
- AI assistants that can plan, reflect, and adapt
- More realistic simulations of intelligent behavior

These systems are increasingly used in:
- AI assistants
- Autonomous research agents
- Workflow automation
- Coding agents
- Decision-support systems
